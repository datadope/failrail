More reliable train services? Get Real

Contrary to SMRT's glowing report that train faults have been halved on the North-South-East-West lines (NSEWL), our data shows that there has not been any significant reduction in the number of service disruptions nor delay hours over the same period (June - August 2012).

Key observations:

  • There wasn't any significant reduction in train and track faults since May.
  • August was a particularly bad month for the NSEWL.
  • On the East West Line, SMRT has failed to meet the 99.5% System Service Availability (SSA) standard every month since December 2011, with exceptions in July and September. The North South Line dipped below the 99.5% SSA in August.
  • Even if train service withdrawals have been halved as SMRT has claimed, the impact to commuters (in terms of time) has worsened as June and August saw longer delay-hours than May.

There has been a curious spike in the number of emergency button activations since September 12, where there can be as many as 9 activations in a single day!
